# Day 10

[Puzzle description.](https://adventofcode.com/2023/day/10)

This is the first properly difficult one this year, in that I needed some time to think of a way to
do it for part 2.

My first instinct was an approach where you start on an outside tile (which you can find by looking
for a ground tile along the edge), and recursively mark all outside tiles, then count the remaining
ground tiles. That would've worked, but was complicated by the "squeezing between pipes" rule. A
recursive method like that would have to find places where it could squeeze between pipes, follow
the gap, and continue on the other side. The logic for that wasn't super trivial.

My next thought was that maybe I could mark every tile on the inside of a corner piece as "inside",
but even the trivial example disproves that as a possibility; those tiles aren't guaranteed to be
inside.

What I finally came up with is that we follow the loop, and mark the ground pieces on either side
of the pipe as "side 1" and "side 2", consistently keeping 1 and 2 on the same side of the pipe
depending on the direction we're moving in. One of those two is the inside, and one the outside,
but we don't know which is which.

Then we check the whole grid, looking for tiles marked as either side, and mark its ground neighbors
the same. Repeat until there are no more new tiles to mark. Determine which side is the outside by
checking which one is found on the edge of the grid, then count the tiles marked with the other side,
which is the inside.

Not sure if it's the best way of doing it, but it works.
